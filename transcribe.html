Transcribe

SoTranscribe is the first paid offering of Sovereign Switch — privacy-respecting transcription, five hosting tiers, two processing profiles, end-to-end encryption between your machine and whichever server you choose.

Status — first paid offering, in transfer. SoTranscribe currently runs on the QuestHub server. We're moving the codebase into the open and the service onto the /transcribe path on this domain. Pricing on this page is being calibrated against our own real processing data; everything marked (calibrating) will firm up before we charge anyone.

Why a paid offering exists on a sovereignty site

The information on this site stays free. SoTranscribe is the working tooling that funds the rest of the project — a service whose business model is your subscription, not your data. That is the same rubric we apply to every provider we recommend on Methodology; we hold ourselves to it too.

Transcription is also the right first product, because it is one of the most common ways non-techies leak sensitive content today: meeting recordings, voice notes, interviews, therapy reflections, walking memos — routinely sent to U.S. cloud transcribers without a second thought. The economic engine is therefore also the closest thing to a daily-driver sovereignty practice for most readers.

How a file moves through the system

Every file follows the same envelope, no matter which hosting tier you pick. The hosting tier only changes where the decryption-and-processing step happens.

  1. Encrypt locally. Your client encrypts the audio on your machine with a key derived from your account passphrase. The plaintext audio never leaves your device.
  2. Ship encrypted. The ciphertext travels to whichever server tier you selected. In transit it's protected twice — by your passphrase-derived key and by standard transport encryption.
  3. Decrypt on the chosen tier. The server tier you picked decrypts the file in memory, runs the model, produces the transcript. This is the only step where plaintext exists on a machine that isn't yours — the entire point of the tier choice is bounding which machine that is.
  4. Encrypt the result, ship it back. The transcript (text only — much smaller than audio) is encrypted with your key and returned. Only your client can read it.
  5. Server-side wipe. The decrypted audio and any transcript caches are purged from the tier server within minutes. Encrypted artefacts are kept only as long as your retention setting says.

For the “Sensitive” tier on the data-tiers ladder, the answer is still don't digitize this. Transcription means a model decrypts your audio at some point; no commercial offering — including ours — changes that. The question is only under whose roof.

Two processing profiles

You pick a profile per file or as a global default. Switching defaults later is one click; re-running a previously processed file under a stricter profile is also supported.

Profile What it does Trade-off Diarizer
Thorough Highest-quality transcription model the chosen tier supports, full diarization (who spoke when), inline timestamps, glossary fitting against your prior files, optional summary. Slowest. Often 2–3× real-time on the slower tiers. (calibrating) On by default.
Fast Smaller / lighter model, single-stream transcription, light timestamps, no cross-file glossary fitting. Quality close to thorough on clean recordings; degrades faster on noisy or multi-speaker audio. Roughly real-time or better. (calibrating) Off by default; toggle on per file.

Hosting tiers — five choices, one disclosure ladder

The tiers are ordered from most sovereign to most commercial. Choose higher up the ladder when the file would meaningfully damage you if it leaked; choose lower when you just want it processed without becoming a behavioural-profile data point.

No fee to us — your hardware, your time. Speed depends on your hardware. Recommended for orgs with one technical person.

The deliberate design: each tier up reduces who can see plaintext, at the price of speed and / or money. Pick the lowest tier that matches the data — and use the data-tiers ladder to decide what counts as which.

For organisations — the admin-grant flow

Organisations have a different operational reality: the person who decides on the sovereignty stance is not always the person whose recordings need processing. For org accounts we offer a simple takeout flow:

  1. The org administrator signs the SoTranscribe service agreement with us, picks the hosting tier, and grants us a scoped access token to the relevant audio sources (a meeting-recording bucket, a voice-memo folder, a recording-platform export).
  2. We pull on schedule or on demand, run the same envelope as for individual users, and deliver transcripts back to a destination the admin specifies.
  3. Per-team policy controls: which tier each team's audio defaults to, retention horizons, who in the org can read which transcripts.

The takeout flow is intentionally narrow — we touch only the sources the admin names. We do not sit in the broader cloud account; we do not become a permanent agent in the org's identity provider.

What this offering does not claim to fix

Honest scope, in keeping with how we treat every project on this site:

  • Plaintext exists somewhere during processing. The chosen tier server decrypts your audio in memory to run the model. We don't have a way around that, and neither does anyone else — if the model has to read it, the model has to read it. The tier system bounds where that happens.
  • The model itself is a counterparty. Even local open-weight models could in principle be backdoored. We document model provenance per tier and update when the underlying models change. See the upcoming model-research series under Methodology.
  • Hardware management engines apply here too. Any commercial-x86 tier server has the same Intel ME / AMD PSP exposure as your laptop. Tier 1 (self-host on your own libreboot/coreboot or RISC-V hardware) is the only way to escape that, and we deliberately publish that as a tier.
  • State actors with judicial leverage on a tier-3 / tier-4 / tier-5 site. A Swiss court order against the Swiss tier, served while a job is decrypted in memory, is on the table. We don't pretend otherwise. Tier 1 and tier 2 raise the bar; tier 5 deliberately does not.
